# Recommendation Memo
**To:** Leadership  
**From:** Radha Manasa Peesapati  
**Date:** 28 June 2026  
**Re:** New Onboarding Campaign — Launch Decision

---

## Summary
A significant improvement in paid conversion was observed in the treatment group (7.04% vs 3.19%, p=0.0005). A phased launch is recommended starting with Mobile users in North and South regions.

---

## Business Problem
The new onboarding campaign was tested to see if more users could be converted to paid plans. The decision is whether to launch to all users, a segment, or not at all. Refund rate and support tickets were monitored as risk indicators.

---

## North Star Metric
**Paid Conversion Rate** was selected as the primary metric as it directly measures revenue impact. Trial start rate and engagement score were treated as supporting metrics.

---

## Key Results

| Metric | Control | Treatment | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Users | 690 | 710 | — |
| Paid Conversion Rate | 3.19% | 7.04% | +3.85% |
| Avg Revenue per User | $51.97 | $54.25 | +$2.28 |
| Refund Rate | 0.00% | 0.42% | +0.42% |
| Support Ticket Rate | 0.22 | 0.37 | +0.15 |
| Engagement Score | 57.03 | 62.94 | +5.91 |

---

## Hypothesis Test
One-tailed z-test was run on paid conversion rate. Z-statistic: 3.26, P-value: 0.0005. Result is significant — null hypothesis rejected.

---

## Guardrail Check
Refund rate increased from 0% to 0.42% and support tickets rose slightly. Neither is at an alarming level but both should be tracked post-launch. Days to convert improved (8.86 → 6.40 days).

---

## Segment Insights
North region showed strongest lift (+5.41%). West region and Social traffic showed weak or negative lift — these need separate review before full inclusion.

---

## Recommendation
**Phased launch.** Start with Mobile users in North and South regions, monitor for 4 weeks, then expand if metrics stay stable.

---

## Risks
- Refund rate was zero in control but non-zero in treatment — monitor for 60 days
- Small conversion counts (22 vs 50) — results need post-launch validation
- One cohort only — seasonal effects not accounted for
